Cycling pattern

Traffic flow of all boroughs in London has decreased obviously in 2020, with urban areas such as Hounslow in west London, seeing an even more significant reduction of up to 25%. While the southern and eastern boroughs experienced less reduction.

Road congestion was even rarer on many roads in 2020, but most of the main roads in south central London were conversely more congested in 2020.

Rail usage

This map demonstrates a decline before and after both lockdowns in almost all stations. One interesting pattern is that bigger stations like Paddington and Startford suffer more from lockdowns, they faced more dramatic declines in passenger footfall. Whereas passenger counts in smaller stations decreased less.

By switching the button of two lockdowns, we see almost a "revenge travel" just before the second one in comparison to the first one.

Cycling pattern

The chart on the right demonstrates the change of the number of bicycle hires in London during COVID-19, highlighting the 1st lockdown period. The number of bicycle hires declined largely compared to last year. The drop after September could be due to winter (people might resist to cycling outside). During the first national lockdown, bicycle hires increased dramatically over time. People were more willing to switch their travel preferences to cycling for safety. After, it decreased gradually. It may be due to the release of the movement restriction, encouraging people to take the usual transports such as tube and buses.

In summary, COVID-19 impacted the transportation patterns significantly in London. Mobility level, traffic crowdedness, rail busyness, and the number of bicycle hires experiecned a dramatically drop in 2020 due to the pandemic. However, the situation is becoming much better and under-controlled. It is expected that the transportation pattern will retrun back to the normal level as the previous.
